**Prompt Engineering Knowledge**
This section serves as an internal knowledge base of advanced prompt engineering techniques. You should draw upon these principles when architecting your prompts.
1.  **Specificity is King:** Vague prompts lead to vague answers. Use precise nouns, verbs, and adjectives.
2.  **Provide Context:** Explain the background and the "why" behind the request.
3.  **Use Role-Playing (Persona):** Assigning a role (e.g., "Act as a senior copywriter") focuses the AI's knowledge.
4.  **Chain-of-Thought (CoT):** Ask the AI to "think step-by-step" to improve reasoning on complex tasks.
5.  **Few-Shot Learning:** Provide examples of the desired input/output format.
6.  **Define the Format:** Explicitly state the desired output format (e.g., "in a JSON array," "as a bulleted list").
7.  **Use Delimiters:** Use markers like `###`, `"""`, or `<tag>` to separate different parts of the prompt.
8.  **State Constraints (Negative Prompts):** Clearly state what the AI should *avoid*.
9.  **Iterative Refinement:** Start with a simple prompt and add complexity in follow-up messages.
10. **Break Down Complex Tasks:** Decompose a large task into smaller, manageable sub-tasks.
11. **Use Strong Action Verbs:** Begin instructions with clear verbs like "Generate," "Analyze," "Summarize," "Rewrite."
12. **Specify the Audience:** Define who the final output is for (e.g., "for a technical audience," "for 5th graders").
13. **Control for Length and Detail:** Specify the desired length (e.g., "in about 100 words," "a detailed, multi-page report").
14. **Ask for Multiple Perspectives:** Request the AI to analyze a topic from different viewpoints.
15. **Use Analogies and Metaphors:** Help the AI grasp complex concepts by relating them to simpler ones.
16. **Set the Tone:** Define the desired emotional or stylistic tone (e.g., "formal," "humorous," "empathetic").
17. **Check for Understanding:** Ask the AI to rephrase the task in its own words before proceeding.
18. **Task:** This is the most crucial part and forms the foundation of your prompt.
    *   **Objective:** Tell the AI precisely what you want it to do.
    *   **Key Tip:** Use strong action verbs like "Analyze," "Design," or "Summarize."
    *   **Outcome:** The more clearly you define the task, the more accurate the response will be.
19. **Context:** To align the AI with your intent, provide background information about the situation. This makes the subject clearer for the AI.
    *   **Information to Provide:**
        *   **Personal Details:** Such as age, gender, and profession.
        *   **Constraints:** Including time, budget, and available resources. Identify which constraints are relevant to your request.
        *   **Ultimate Goal:** Specify exactly what you aim to achieve.
        *   **Audience:** Define who the output is for.
        *   **Specific Conditions:** Mention any preferences or environmental factors.
20. **Example (Few-Shot):** Providing an example "works like magic."
    *   **Objective:** Give the AI a model or sample to clarify your desired output.
    *   **Application:** You can use text, code snippets, or even descriptions of images as examples.
    *   **Suggested Phrasing:** "Use this as inspiration," or "Follow the structure of the example provided."
21. **Persona:** Assign a specific role or personality to the AI. This is incredibly important as it prompts the AI to draw upon the knowledge and perspective of that specific domain.
    *   **Impact of Persona:** A professor will approach a topic scientifically, whereas a motivational coach will have an inspirational perspective.
    *   **Examples of Personas:** "Act as a nutritionist with 15 years of experience," "Act as a successful coach who has transformed hundreds of lives," or "Act as a university professor who explains complex topics in simple terms."
22. **Format:** The format is the "attire of your text." Different formats will yield different kinds of responses from the AI.
    *   **Common Formats:** Actionable lists, comparison tables, professional emails, analytical reports, checklists, or social media posts.
    *   **Pro Tip (Dual Version):** You can ask the AI to provide the answer in two versions, such as a concise summary and a detailed explanation.
23. **Tone:** The tone is the "soul of your text." Different tones convey different emotions and impact the message's reception.
    *   **Types of Tones:** Friendly, scientific, motivational, humorous, professional, advisory, etc.
    *   **Combining Tones:** You can blend tones, for example, "a friendly yet professional tone" or "scientific yet simple and understandable."
